📖 Novel Reading Status Guide

Understanding how Ranobe Gemini tracks your reading progress with automatic and manual status management.

Core Concept

The novel status system distinguishes between author progress and your progress. A novel's completion status depends on whether the author has finished writing it.

Primary Statuses

📖 Reading

Reading

You are actively reading this novel and are behind the latest chapter. More content is available for you to read.

Transitions to: Up to Date (when you reach latest chapter)

✨ Up to Date

Up to Date

You have read all available chapters. This status applies to:

  • Ongoing novels: Author hasn't completed the series yet
  • Completed novels: You've finished the entire series

Transitions to:

  • Reading (when new chapters are released)
  • Completed (if you mark as completed and author has finished)
✅ Completed

Completed

You have finished reading this novel. Only available for novels completed by the author.

For ongoing/incomplete novels: Use "Up to Date" instead to indicate you're current with latest chapters.

Transitions to: Reading (if new chapters appear after author continues)

📋 Plan to Read

Plan to Read

You want to read this novel in the future but haven't started yet.

Manual status: Change this whenever you're ready to start reading.

⏸️ On Hold

On Hold

You're pausing this novel temporarily. No automatic transitions occur.

Manual status: Change back to Reading when you're ready to continue.

❌ Dropped

Dropped

You've decided not to continue reading this novel.

Manual status: Use this for novels you no longer wish to track.

🏷️ Reading Lists

Reading Lists (Badges, not statuses)

Reading lists are parallel labels that can be applied to any novel regardless of its primary status.

  • 🔁 Rereading (predefined)
  • ⭐ Favourites (predefined)
  • Custom lists (for example: R18)

Feature highlight

🏷️ Reading-List Badges

Reading-list badges are available from each novel's status dropdown and can be toggled without changing the primary reading status.

A novel can stay 📖 Reading while also having 🔁 Rereading and ⭐ Favourites badges.

How it works:

  • Badges are independent from reading statuses
  • Both predefined lists are available in the status dropdown
  • Users can add custom list names in settings data
  • Useful for thematic grouping (for example: R18)

Perfect for: Re-read campaigns, favourites curation, and custom shelf-like personal groupings.

Diagram Elements

  • Reading: Active reading in progress.
  • Up to Date: Caught up with latest published chapter.
  • Completed: Finished by reader, usually for finished series.
  • Plan to Read: Tracked but not started.
  • On Hold: Temporarily paused.
  • Dropped: Intentionally stopped.

🎯 For Completed Novels (Author Finished)

When you read the final chapter of a completed novel, the status automatically changes to Up to Date. You can then manually set it to Completed.

🚀 For Ongoing Novels (Author Still Writing)

When you reach the latest chapter of an ongoing novel, it becomes Up to Date. If the author releases new chapters later, it automatically reverts to Reading.

📝 For One-shots (Single Chapter)

One-shot stories remain under your manual control. The extension doesn't automatically change status since there's no "new chapters" scenario.

⏸️ Manual Statuses (No Auto-Transition)

The following statuses never automatically change:

  • Plan to Read: Changes only when you manually update
  • On Hold: Stays until you manually change it
  • Dropped: Permanent until manually changed

Real-World Examples

Example 1: Ongoing Web Novel

Scenario: You're reading "The Endless Tower" on Wuxia site. Author is still writing.

  1. You start reading → Status: Reading
  2. You catch up to the latest chapter → Status auto-changes to: Up to Date
  3. Author releases 5 new chapters → Status auto-changes back to: Reading
  4. You read those chapters and catch up again → Status: Up to Date

Note: Status never becomes "Completed" because the author hasn't finished writing.

Example 2: Completed Web Novel

Scenario: You're reading "The Lost Kingdom" which is fully written by the author.

  1. You start reading → Status: Reading
  2. You reach the final chapter → Status auto-changes to: Up to Date
  3. You manually mark as done → Status: Completed
  4. No new chapters can appear → Status remains: Completed

Example 3: One-shot Short Story

Scenario: You read a single-chapter short story.

  1. You read the story → Status: Reading (stays here)
  2. You manually change to: Completed
  3. No automatic transitions occur since it's a one-shot
  4. Status stays as you set it: Completed

Example 4: Series on Hiatus

Scenario: You're reading a series, but the author goes on hiatus.

  1. You read latest chapter → Status: Up to Date
  2. Author announces hiatus (no new chapters) → Status: Up to Date (stays)
  3. You want to pause → Manually change to: On Hold
  4. When author resumes → You manually change back to: Reading

Note: The extension can detect new chapters but respects your "On Hold" status if you set it.

Quick Reference

Status Auto-Changes? Use When...
📖 Reading ✅ Yes Behind the latest chapter
✨ Up to Date ✅ Yes Caught up (ongoing or completed novels)
✅ Completed ❌ No* Finished reading a completed novel
📋 Plan to Read ❌ No Want to read but haven't started
⏸️ On Hold ❌ No Temporarily pausing
❌ Dropped ❌ No Not continuing anymore
🏷️ Reading Lists (badge layer) N/A Parallel tags like 🔁 Rereading / ⭐ Favourites / custom labels

*Only if author releases new chapters after completion

📌 Key Takeaway

The status system intelligently separates author progress from your reading progress. For ongoing series, "Up to Date" means you're caught up—not that the series is complete. Only use "Completed" for series that the author has actually finished. This keeps your reading status accurate and meaningful.
