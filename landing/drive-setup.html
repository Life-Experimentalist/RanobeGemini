Step-by-step OAuth setup

Getting Your Google OAuth Credentials

This guide creates a free OAuth 2.0 client for your personal use. It takes about 5 minutes and requires a Google account.

🔒 Why do I need OAuth credentials?

Google requires each user to create their own OAuth client for apps that access Google Drive. This keeps your data completely private — Ranobe Gemini never has access to your credentials or your Drive data. Everything runs locally in your browser.

  1. 1

    Open Google Cloud Console

    Go to console.cloud.google.com and sign in with your Google account.

  2. 2

    Create a New Project

    Click the project selector at the top of the page, then click New Project.

    • Give it any name you like, e.g. Ranobe Gemini Backup
    • Leave "Organisation" as "No organisation" if prompted
    • Click Create and wait a moment for it to be ready
  3. 3

    Enable the Google Drive API

    With your new project selected, navigate to APIs & Services → Library from the left menu. Search for Google Drive API and click Enable.

    💡 Make sure you are in the correct project before enabling APIs.

  4. 4

    Configure the OAuth Consent Screen

    Go to APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen. Choose External user type and click Create.

    • App name: anything, e.g. Ranobe Gemini Backup
    • User support email: your Gmail address
    • Developer contact email: your Gmail address

    Click Save and Continue through the Scopes, Test users, and Summary pages without changing anything.

    💡 The app will remain in "Testing" mode, which is fine for personal use — it allows up to 100 test users with no Google review needed.

  5. 5

    Create OAuth Client Credentials

    Go to APIs & Services → Credentials. Click + Create Credentials → OAuth client ID.

    • Application type: Desktop app
    • Name: anything, e.g. Ranobe Gemini Desktop

    Click Create. A dialog shows your Client ID and Client Secret — you can dismiss it and download the JSON file instead.

  6. 6

    Add Authorised Redirect URIs

    After creating the credentials, click the pencil ✏️ edit icon next to your new OAuth client. Under Authorised redirect URIs, add both of these URLs for whichever browser you use:

    Click Save after adding the URIs. You can add both even if you only use one browser.

    💡 If you see "Invalid redirect URI" errors during auth, double-check that these URIs are entered exactly as shown.

  7. 7

    Download the Credentials JSON

    On the Credentials page, find your OAuth client and click the ⬇️ download icon on the right side of the row. This downloads a file named client_secret_*.json.

    ⚠️ Keep this file safe. Anyone with your Client Secret can make requests on your behalf. Do not share it publicly.

  8. 8

    Enter Credentials in Ranobe Gemini

    Open the Ranobe Gemini Library → Settings → Backup → Google Drive Backups → OAuth Setup. There are two methods:

    📋 Method A — Paste JSON (Recommended)

    Open the downloaded client_secret_*.json file in a text editor, copy all the contents, and paste into the Paste credentials JSON box. Click Parse JSON then Save to Storage.

    ✏️ Method B — Manual Entry

    Copy the client_id and client_secret values from your JSON file and paste them individually into the Client ID and Client Secret fields. Click Save Credentials.

    After saving, click Connect Google Drive and follow the browser's OAuth popup to authorise access. You will see "🟢 Connected" once complete.

    🎉 Done! Your library will now back up to Google Drive automatically whenever it changes.

How Rolling Backups Work

Retention Policy: The extension maintains up to 7 rolling backups. When a new backup is created and the limit is reached, the oldest backup is automatically deleted.

Backup Frequency: Backups are created:

  • Every 24 hours (automatic)
  • On library updates (status changes, novel additions)
  • On manual request (quick backup button)

File Naming: Backups use timestamps like ranobe-gemini-backup-2026-02-16.json so you can easily identify and restore from specific dates.

✨ Enable in Library → Advanced Settings → Backup Mode

Support

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ How much space will backups take?

Library backups are lightweight JSON files (typically 50KB-500KB). A year of daily backups uses approximately 20-100MB of your Google Drive storage.

🔐 Is my data safe?

Yes. Data is stored in your personal Google Drive, encrypted in transit (HTTPS), and requires OAuth2 authorization to access. Even the extension developers cannot see your data.

🚫 Can I revoke access?

Yes. Go to Google Account → Apps & Services → Ranobe Gemini and disconnect anytime. This immediately stops backups and doesn't delete existing backups from your Drive.

📥 How do I restore a backup?

In the extension settings, click "Restore from Backup" and select the backup file from your Google Drive. Your library will be restored to that exact point in time. You can restore from any backup anytime.

📱 What if I have multiple devices?

Backups are stored centrally in your Google Drive. Install the extension on any device and authorize it to access the same backups. Your library syncs across all devices automatically.

🗑️ What happens to backups if I revoke access?

Backups stay in your Google Drive and are never deleted. You can reconnect later and restore from them. Or manually delete them from your Drive anytime you want to free up space.

🔄 How often are backups created?

Backups are created automatically every 24 hours. Additional backups are created immediately when you update your library (add novel, change status, update notes, etc.).

💬 Does the extension upload logs to Drive?

Yes, error logs and debug information are optionally uploaded to assist with troubleshooting. You can disable this in the settings anytime. No personal data is included in logs.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

⚠️ "Authorization Failed"

Clear your browser cache and try again. Make sure pop-ups are allowed for this site. Try using a different Google account if the issue persists. You may also need to refresh the browser.

⚠️ "Backup Not Syncing"

Check that the extension has Drive permissions in your browser. Try re-authorizing by clicking "Setup Google Drive" again in the extension settings. Verify your internet connection is active.

⚠️ "Storage Quota Exceeded"

Delete old backup files from your Google Drive or upgrade your Drive storage plan. You can safely delete old backups to free up space. Keep the most recent few backups.

⚠️ "Popup shows Disconnected after setup"

Refresh the popup and try opening it again. If it still shows disconnected, verify your internet connection and try re-authorizing the extension.

⚠️ "Can't find my backup files in Drive"

Backups are stored in a "Ranobe Gemini Backups" folder in your Google Drive root. Check that folder directly. If not present, backups haven't been created yet - try creating a manual backup first.
